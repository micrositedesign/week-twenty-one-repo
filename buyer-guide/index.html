Buyer Guide

Get Your Domain On!

.COM

A .com domain is the most trusted, well-established, most sought after top-level domain out there. This also means good names are harder to find for a reasonable price. Unless you are a non-profit, or another specific type of company or organization, having an online identity with a .com extension can be the most preferred for you and your online identity.

Keywords

Using keywords to describe your organization helps people find you more easily. It also plays a part in getting your domain ranked higher on Google Search Results Pages. Coupled with high quality content and an exceptional user experience, a domain with keywords in it will make your organization stand out online, and may lead to more successful conversions.

Short

Long, complex domain names may have your potential site visitors misspelling or mistyping your domain name. It is also harder to remember longer domain names. The ideal domain length is 12 characters or less, but you can have a domain up to 15 characters long if you need it. Try not to go over 15 characters because certain social media sites don’t support social media handles longer than 15 characters.

Pronunciation

Being able to tell someone your website address when communicating verbally is an ideal domain to have. You never know when you need to give someone your website verbally, and someone listening to you share the web address should be easy for them to understand. You can ask people to pronounce your domain name when they read it to see if it rolls off the lips, or if it needs to be revised. Word-of-mouth is still a huge way for people to find your website.

Spelling

The spelling of a domain can be looked at from different points of view. On the one hand, funny spellings, or words that can be spelled different ways can make it difficult for people to find your website, and misspellings can make your site look suspicious. On the other hand, unconventional ways of spelling your company can make your company stand out, especially if it is catchy. Just remember people need to type the address easily, and word-of-mouth may have people wondering how to spell the domain.

Brandable

A brand needs to be unique, and your domain name should be a reflection of your brand. Try to make the domain you choose catchy and memorable. Something creative and innovative is always better than something generic. Your brand tells your organization’s story. An ideal brand uses a name that may have no specific meaning on its own, it is easy to memorize, it is unique, it is trustworthy and it is easy to pronounce. Your brand is your foundation.

No Hyphens or Numbers

Numbers and hyphens make a domain less appealing. Having them in your domain may make your domain prone to typos. Someone who hears your domain may not know if the 3 in your domain should be spelled out three or if it is the number. Numbers and hyphens in domains are also known naming conventions for hacker and spam sites. Which domain seems more legitimate, https://home-depot.com or https://homedepot.com?

Easy to Type

A domain that is easy to type will lead to more visitors viewing your site. Try to think of some websites that you visit on a regular basis. Do they have a domain name that is easy to type? Probably, yes. A good way to test if your domain name is easy to type is to ask a friend or relative to type it for you. If you have to give them multiple hints, the domain is probably too complex, and it may not be the right one for you. Also avoid using words that can be spelled different ways.

Avoid Double Lettering

Double lettering your domain name makes the domain more prone to people mistyping it, and typos can lead to your site losing traffic, or it may even have people going to your competitor’s site. How easy is a domain like https://classsubjects.com to read and to type?

Target Your Location

You may want to consider using your location in your domain name if your other domain choices are already taken. If your organization operates in a particular part of the world, including a city, state, or province in your domain may be beneficial for your online presence. If your business is in Seattle, Washington, and you searched for a domain name using a few keywords and all of the keyword domains were taken, include Seattle, or Seattle, Washington to the domain search. There are also top level domains specific to locations like .us, .ca, .de and .co.uk.

First Impressions

Your online presence is many times the very first impression someone has with your organization. Domain names can lead to an impression of your company for the better, and sometimes for the worse. This is not to say that a domain name does this on its own. The user experience on the actual website can also lead to good and bad impressions. Committing to a domain name has positive and negative connotations felt years down the road. If your brand is about high quality, the domain name and website should also reflect this ideal.

Organizational Expansion

A domain name that is catchy, unique, and memorable will lead to improved user experiences, but you do not want the domain name to limit any options you may have for longer term organizational objectives. For example, if you remodel bathrooms, and your domain name is seattlebathroomremodeling.com, this will limit your ability to branch out and offer other remodeling services. Always keep in mind that if your domain name is very narrow in scope, using it to offer other services may be very difficult to accomplish.

Trademark

Checking to see if a domain name is trademarked is a really good idea. You also want to avoid legal issues with potential domains that already have trademarks associated with them. Trademarking your own domain name is also a good business strategy because it makes you the trademark owner of the name. Keep in mind certain names can’t be trademarked, and it may help if you get a legal consult regarding the trademark. You can also do a free trademark search at the United States Patent and Trademark Office website.

SEO

Search Engine Optimization hosts an array of benefits because good SEO increases your page ranking on popular search engines like Google. Better rankings leads to more people finding your website. Domain names are only one factor search engines use to rank pages, and there are 100s of other factors that influence it, but having a URL with potential keywords in it helps tremendously with search engine rankings. There is more to it than that, but your domain name is a good start.

Social Media

Keeping your brand consistent across multiple online channels is definitely something you want to aim at. Channels you will definitely want to have established is with your social media presence. Each popular social media channel gives you a brandable URL. So, if you want to find Target on Facebook and Instagram, you can follow them on @target. Having a social media handle that coincides with your domain name makes the name familiar, reliable, and give a better online user experience. People may even check your social media presence before they check out your website.

Thesaurus

A really good way to get your creative juices flowing when it comes to picking a domain name is to use an old-fashioned thesaurus. A thesaurus will help you find other words that are similar to a word you are looking for in your potential domain name search. Finding cheaper available domain names that are memorable, short, and brandable is not always an easy task, so utilizing your available resources will help you achieve your domain name goals.

Name Generators

If you are having trouble finding an acceptable domain name, it may be a good idea to try out some domain name generators. These free tools take a keyword that you give it and comes up with numerous possible names you can use. Try out different keywords to see what results you get. To get you started, try out Nameboy, Wordoid, or Domain Hole.

Domain History

Checking your domains history is definitely something you should do. You don’t want to end up with a domain that has a dark past. The first tool to use is the Wayback Machine. This website takes snapshots of websites throughout history. You can go back years to see if your website was every used maleficently. Another tool to use is Who Is. This website search will allow you to see partial information for the current domain owner, and with a paid service you can get a history report giving you information from the very beginning of a domain.

Registrar

A registrar is the company you use to register a domain name on your behalf. You are able to perform free searches on a registrar’s website for your potential domain name. If you find a domain name you want, you create an account with the registrar and purchase it. Different registrars offer domains for different prices, and they may include different benefits for registering. Use respectable and trustworthy registrars so there are fewer problems down the road.

Act Quickly

Domain names come and go very quickly. If you find a domain name that has not been registered, and you want it, it is in your best interest to purchase it sooner than later. Remember, as long as the domain is available, anyone may register it at any time, and this includes anyone around the world. Keep in mind once you register a domain, that registration is good for a year. This means you have a whole year to decide if you want to keep the domain. It also means if you decide to get rid of it you are only out the cost of registration which is not a very big investment. Of course, premium domains are a different story.

Outside Agency

This recommendation may cause some controversy, but Domainers Anonymous sees it only one way. When you purchase hosting from a web host, a lot of times the web host will throw in a free domain name registration as part of the web hosting package. As tempting as this may be, we think it is a better choice to register the domain name yourself. This gives you ultimate control over the domain, and should you leave the web hosting company for someone else in the future, you do not need to worry about issues with transferring the domain away from the old host.

ID Protection

It is best practice when registering a new domain to enable what is called ID Protection. As a domain name owner, your personal information including your name, address, telephone number and email address is recorded with ICANN, the governing body of domain names, and this personal information can be viewed publicly by anyone with a computing device and internet access. ID Protection conceals this personally identifiable information and replaces it with the registrar’s information. In some cases, having your information viewable is a better option.

Autorenewal

Your domain name is typically registered for a period of 12 months, but this can be extended when first registering the domain with the registrar, or during any period the domain name is in good standing with the registrar. Once the time period of the registration is up, and the domain was not renewed, the domain typically goes into a grace period before the domain is released back into the domain pool or auctioned off. Sometimes the domain registrant is not aware their domain registration is set to expire leading to headaches and potential website access issues. It is recommended that you set the domain to auto-renew by having a credit card on file with the registrar.
